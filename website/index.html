What is OpenData Tool?

The **OpenData Tool** is designed for scientists and researchers who need to publish their data in the RODBUK repository. Preparing metadata can be a tedious, technical hurdleâ€”this tool automates the "boring parts" by scanning your research directories and using AI to suggest titles, authors, abstracts, and keywords directly from your papers and data files.

Read-Only Safety

The tool never modifies your original research files. Your data remains untouched and secure.

AI-Powered

Extracts metadata from LaTeX, Word, PDFs, and scientific formats like VASP, DICOM, and HDF5.

Interactive Chat

Refine your metadata through a simple conversation. Tell the tool what's important, and it learns.

How to Use It

1

Launch & Setup

Open the app. It will launch a small control window and your web browser. Sign in with Google to enable AI features.

2

Scan Directory

Point the tool to your research project folder. It will perform a lightning-fast scan to identify relevant files.

3

Review & Chat

The AI will propose metadata. You can edit it manually or use the Chat window to say: "Add Prof. Smith as a co-author" or "Summarize the abstract from the LaTeX file."

4

Package

Once satisfied, click 'Generate Package'. A ZIP file ready for RODBUK will be created in your workspace.

Current Focus: User Acceptance Testing (UAT)

Gathering feedback from domain professionals (you!) to refine the AI's scientific reasoning and extraction accuracy.

Testing Procedure

We need your feedback on the "vibe" and accuracy of the tool. Please follow these steps:

  • Variety: Try scanning different types of projects (Physics, Medical, General Office docs).
  • AI Accuracy: Does the AI correctly identify the "Main Paper"? Are the authors correctly extracted?
  • Conversation: Try to "teach" the tool. (e.g., "In this project, .out files are always energy logs"). Does it remember?
  • UI/UX: Is the dashboard intuitive? Can you find your way without a manual?
  • Stability: Does the app crash on very large directories? (It shouldn't!)

Reporting Bugs: Please use the /bug command in the app or contact the development team with your log files.

Current Limitations (Beta)

Maximum 5-message memory for AI chat context.
Only LaTeX, Docx, and specific Physics formats are deeply analyzed.
Remote access (Mobile) requires manual "Enable" for security.
Final packaging currently limited to ZIP format.
