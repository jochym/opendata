Documentation

Concept & Mission

The OpenData Tool is a Proof of Concept (PoC) exploring the use of Generative AI for automated scientific metadata extraction.

Our goal is to determine if AI can reliably "read" a researcher's working directory and draft a high-quality metadata record for the RODBUK repository with minimal human intervention.

  • Status: Early Alpha / Technology Demonstrator.
  • Scope: Metadata drafting and documentation packaging only. Research data handling is NOT yet implemented.
  • Target Audience: Domain experts willing to test the boundaries of AI interpretation in Physics and Medical Physics.

Read-Only Safety

The tool never modifies your original research files. Your data remains untouched and secure.

No Telemetry: We do not track your activity. The code is open source and free to inspect.

AI-Powered

Extracts metadata from LaTeX, Word, PDFs, and scientific formats like VASP, DICOM, and HDF5.

Interactive Chat

Refine your metadata through a simple conversation. Tell the tool what's important, and it learns.

How to Use It

Access Requirement

To use the AI features (Google Gemini), you must be added to our internal testing team. Please contact Pawel T. Jochym to request access. You will need a valid Google Account.

Privacy Note: Your file snippets are sent directly to your authenticated Google Gemini instance. The authors of this tool have no access to your data, metadata, or chat history in any form.

1

Launch & Setup

Open the app. It will launch a small control window and your web browser. Sign in with Google to enable AI features.

2

Scan Directory

Point the tool to your research project folder. It will perform a lightning-fast scan to identify relevant files.

3

Review & Chat

The AI will propose metadata. You can edit it manually or use the Chat window to say: "Add Prof. Smith as a co-author" or "Summarize the abstract from the LaTeX file."

4

Package

Once satisfied, click 'Generate Package'. A ZIP file ready for RODBUK will be created in your workspace.
